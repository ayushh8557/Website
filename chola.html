Chole Kulche

Chhole Kulche is a popular North Indian dish that is enjoyed by people of all ages. It is a combination of spicy chickpeas (chhole) and soft bread made from refined flour (kulche). The dish is usually served with a side of onions, green chilies, and a tangy tamarind chutney. There are many variations of the recipe, but the basic ingredients include boiled chickpeas, onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. The chickpeas are cooked in a tomato-based gravy and then garnished with fresh coriander leaves.

prep time

1hrs

cook time

30min

serving

6 servings

instructions

step 1

Boil the chickpeas: Soak the chickpeas overnight and then boil them in a pressure cooker with water, salt, a pinch of baking soda, and turmeric. Once boiled, mash the chickpeas with a spatula.

step 2

Add the spices: In a large bowl, mix the mashed chickpeas with chopped onions and tomatoes, chaat masala, salt, red chilli powder, and lemon juice.

step 3

Toast the kulchas: Toast the kulchas on a hot tawa with some butter until they are golden brown. Serve hot with the chhole.
