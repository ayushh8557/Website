Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian sweet that is enjoyed by people of all ages. It is made from milk solids and sugar, and is usually flavored with rose water and cardamom powder. The dough is made by mixing milk powder or khoya with flour, baking powder, and ghee. The dough is then rolled into small balls and deep-fried until golden brown. The fried balls are then soaked in a sugar syrup flavored with rose water and cardamom powder.

prep time

2.5hrs

cook time

1.5hrs

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Make the dough: In a mixing bowl, add milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and ghee. Slowly add water and knead the mixture until it forms a smooth dough.

step 2

Shape the dough: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls. Make sure there are no cracks on the surface of the balls.

step 3

Fry and soak: Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the balls until they turn golden brown. Remove them from the oil and soak them in sugar syrup for at least 30 minutes.
